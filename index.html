Finding Home in Unexpected Places

Posted: Coming Soon

The moment I realized that "home" isn't just a place‚Äîit's the feeling of belonging that comes from connecting with students and communities anywhere in the world. It hit me around a table in downtown Daegu surrounded by fellow teachers from Australia, Canada, the UK, and South Africa - we called ourselves "The Muppets" because we were all a little crazy for leaving everything behind to teach on the other side of the world. Despite our different accents and backgrounds, we became family. We shared homesickness over cheap Korean BBQ, celebrated small classroom victories together, and supported each other through the challenges of being foreigners trying to make a difference. In that moment, I understood that home isn't your passport or your childhood bedroom - it's the people who choose to walk this journey with you, no matter how far from "home" you all are..

My Journey at a Glance

From courthouse clerk to global educator - here are the key moments that shaped my path.

2007: The life-changing Cambodia trip that led to my first teaching assignment in Daegu, South Korea
2009-2012: Three transformative years in Pohang, Korea - deepening friendships and cultural immersion
2012-2014: Life in Korea's breadbasket region, Namwon - experiencing rural Korean traditions
2014-2017: Cultural immersion in Dammam, Saudi Arabia - broadening horizons in the Middle East
2018-Present: Special Education teacher in Houston, Texas - applying global insights to support diverse learners
