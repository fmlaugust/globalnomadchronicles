My Journey Timeline

A chronological overview of my travels and teaching experiences around the world.

2005

Mexico - The Travel Bug Awakens

While working at Harris County Courthouse, visited the Yucatan Peninsula. This trip sparked my passion for international travel.

2006

Peru - Full Travel Bug Hits

Tourist exploration of Peru, visiting ancient Incan sites and experiencing Andean culture. The travel bug was now in full effect.

January 2007

Cambodia – Tomb Raider and the Travel Bug

While exploring the ancient temples of Angkor Wat—channeling my inner Tomb Raider—I met a wonderful woman who was a teacher in South Korea. Her stories opened a window into a world I hadn't seriously considered before. The travel bug bit me hard—like a virus with no cure and no hope for recovery. Came back home, started the paperwork, put in my resignation, and the rest was history.

May 2007

First Teaching Assignment - Daegu, South Korea

Started my first international teaching position in Daegu, South Korea with EPIK program. The beginning of my overseas teaching career.

January 2008

Thailand – First Taste of Southeast Asia

Took my first real vacation while teaching in Daegu and headed to Thailand. The vibrant culture, stunning beaches, and spicy street food opened my eyes to a whole new side of travel.

May 2008

USA – Back Home, But Not for Long

Returned stateside to H-Town, thinking I was homesick. Turns out, I wasn't. The travel bug had fully taken hold—there was no going back now.

January 2009 – January 2012

South Korea – Pohang and the Pull of Adventure

Returned to Korea for the next chapter of my journey—this time to the coastal city of Pohang. What began as another teaching contract turned into three unforgettable years filled with deepening friendships, cultural immersion, and even more Korean adventures.

January 2011

Malaysia – Islands, Food, and New Horizons

Traveled to Malaysia during winter break—drawn by its mix of cultures, lush islands, and incredible cuisine. From bustling markets to serene beaches, it was a journey that deepened my love for Southeast Asia.

January 2012

USA – H-Town, but Korea Was Calling

Back in Houston, trying to settle in—but something didn't feel right. The rhythm of life abroad, the adventures, the connections... Korea still echoed in my mind. I knew I wasn't done yet.

May 2012 – May 2014

South Korea – Life in the Breadbasket: Namwon

Moved to Namwon, nestled in Korea's fertile heartland. Surrounded by rolling fields and rich traditions, I experienced a quieter side of Korean life—where agriculture and history meet, and every day felt grounded in something real and timeless.

May 2014 – August 2014

USA – Now for Something Completely Different

Back home, restless and craving new experiences. Like Monty Python said, "And now for something completely different." Ready to dive into a new culture and continent, eager for the next big adventure to shake things up.

September 2014 – August 2017

Saudi Arabia – Three Years in Dammam

Landed in Dammam for a three-year chapter filled with new challenges and rich cultural experiences. Living and working in Saudi Arabia broadened my horizons and deepened my understanding of the Middle East.

2015

Journey to Jordan

Tourist exploration of Jordan, visiting ancient Petra and experiencing Middle Eastern hospitality.

August 2017

Jordan & UAE – Final Adventures Abroad

As my overseas teaching journey neared its close, I returned to Jordan for a deeper experience—this time camping in the desert and discovering a new, quieter side of Petra. Before heading home, I extended my stay with time in the Emirates, soaking in the contrasting modernity and tradition of the region.

September 2017

USA – Coming Full Circle

Returned to the United States after years abroad, taking time to regroup and reflect on all I had seen, learned, and experienced during my travels. This chapter marked the close of my international teaching journey and the beginning of a new phase grounded in global perspective and personal growth.

2018 – Present

USA – Building a Career in Special Education

After returning home, I began subbing and worked toward earning my teaching certification in Texas. I secured my first "real" job as a Special Education teacher in Houston, where I've been applying the rich international experiences and cultural insights gained abroad to support and inspire students with diverse needs.
